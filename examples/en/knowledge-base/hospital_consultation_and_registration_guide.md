# 1. Registration and Appointment Guide
This section covers frequently asked questions regarding how to register, slot release times, and cancellation rules.

## 1.1 Registration Channels
Patients can book appointments through the following channels:
- **WeChat Official Account/Mini Program**: Search for "Huatuo Hospital Official Account" or "Huatuo Hospital Mini Program" for online booking (Recommended).
- **Official APP**: Download the "Huatuo Hospital App" to make appointments.
- **Self-service Kiosks**: Located on every floor (supports ID cards, Social Security cards, and Electronic Medical Insurance vouchers).
- **Manual Window**: Registration and Payment windows on the 1st floor of the Outpatient Hall (Workdays 08:00 - 17:30).
- **Telephone Appointment**: Call 010-12345678 (Workdays 08:00 - 17:30).

## 1.2 Slot Release and Appointment Cycle
- **Release Time**: New appointment slots for the next [7] days are released daily at [20:00] sharp.
- **Appointment Limit**: The same patient can book a maximum of [3] appointments in different departments on the same day.
- **Same-day Registration**: If slots are still available on the day of visit, you can register via the WeChat Mini Program or on-site; if slots are full, it is recommended to book for another date.

## 1.3 Cancellation and No-show Rules
- **Online Cancellation**: Appointments can be cancelled directly through the original booking channel before [23:59] on the day prior to the visit.
- **Same-day Cancellation**: If you need to cancel on the day of the visit, you must handle it at the manual window [30 minutes] before your scheduled time slot.
- **No-show Penalty**: Failure to attend the appointment without prior cancellation is recorded as a "No-show." Accumulating [3] no-shows within half a year will result in a [90]-day restriction on online registration functions.

# 2. Intelligent Triage (Find Department by Symptoms)
*This section is used for the bot to identify patient symptoms and recommend the corresponding department.*

## 2.1 Internal Medicine System
- **Cardiology**: Chest tightness, chest pain, palpitations, heart racing, hypertension (high blood pressure), dizziness.
- **Respiratory Medicine**: Cough, phlegm, coughing up blood, difficulty breathing, asthma, fever with respiratory symptoms.
- **Gastroenterology**: Stomach pain, acid reflux, nausea/vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, black stools, jaundice.
- **Neurology**: Headache, insomnia, facial paralysis, numbness in limbs, convulsions, epilepsy, memory loss.
- **Endocrinology**: Excessive thirst/eating/urination (Diabetes), thyroid enlargement, obesity, gout/high uric acid.

## 2.2 Surgical System
- **General Surgery**: Abdominal pain (appendicitis, cholecystitis, etc.), superficial lumps, thyroid/breast nodules, hernia.
- **Orthopedics**: Fractures, joint pain, low back and leg pain, cervical spondylosis, scoliosis, traumatic injuries.
- **Urology**: Frequent urination, urgent urination, painful urination, hematuria (blood in urine), kidney stones, prostate issues.

## 2.3 Obstetrics, Gynecology, and Pediatrics
- **Gynecology**: Irregular menstruation, painful periods, abnormal vaginal discharge, gynecological inflammation, uterine fibroids, cervical screening.
- **Obstetrics**: Pre-pregnancy consultation, prenatal check-ups, maternity filing, childbirth, postpartum check-ups.
- **Pediatrics**: Fever, cough, diarrhea, or growth retardation in children under 14 years old.

## 2.4 ENT and Other Specialties
- **Ophthalmology (Eye)**: Vision loss, red eyes, eye pain, cataracts, glaucoma, optometry/glasses fitting.
- **Otolaryngology (ENT)**: Tinnitus, hearing loss, nasal congestion, nosebleeds, sore throat, snoring.
- **Stomatology (Dental)**: Toothache, tooth extraction, fillings, orthodontics, bleeding gums.
- **Dermatology (Skin)**: Rash, itching, hair loss, acne, spot removal, skin allergies.

## 2.5 Quick Triage Knowledge
### Burns
For burns, it is recommended to visit **General Surgery**.
### Chest Pain
For chest pain, it is recommended to visit **Cardiology**.
### Hair Loss
For hair loss, it is recommended to visit **Dermatology**.

# 3. Consultation Process and Notes
This section is used to answer questions about the workflow after arriving at the hospital.

## 3.1 Pre-consultation Preparation
- **Required Documents**: Valid ID (ID card/Household Register/Passport), physical Social Security card, or Electronic Medical Insurance voucher.
- **Medical Records**: Previous medical records, recent test reports, medical imaging (X-ray/CT/MRI films).
- **Fasting Requirements**: If you require blood tests (liver function, blood sugar, blood lipids, etc.), abdominal ultrasound, or gastroscopy/colonoscopy, please remain fasting (no food or water for at least 8 hours).

## 3.2 Check-in and Consultation
- **Sign-in/Check-in**: Please arrive at the triage desk of the respective department [30 minutes] before your appointment slot. Use the self-service machine or present your registration voucher to "Check-in."
- **Queueing**: After checking in, please wait in the waiting area. Watch the display screen and listen for the voice announcement. If you miss your turn, you will need to be re-queued.

## 3.3 Payment and Pharmacy
- **Payment Methods**: Supports "In-consultation payment" (scan WeChat/Alipay QR code), self-service kiosk payment, and manual window payment.
- **Pharmacy Process**: After payment, follow the instructions on the guidance slip to the corresponding pharmacy (e.g., Western Medicine Pharmacy, 1st Floor). When your name appears on the screen, go to the designated window to collect your medicine.

# 4. Medical Insurance and Finance
*This section answers questions regarding insurance reimbursement and costs.*

## 4.1 Insurance Policy
- **Local Insurance**: Supports direct settlement for local employee medical insurance and resident medical insurance. Ensure you select "Insurance Payment" during registration and payment.
- **Cross-regional Insurance**: Patients must complete "Cross-regional Medical Treatment Filing" in their place of insurance coverage beforehand. Once successful, you can swipe your card for direct settlement at this hospital.
- **Outpatient Pooling**: Patients eligible for outpatient pooling will automatically enjoy the reimbursement ratio at the time of payment.

## 4.2 Invoices and Statements
- **Electronic Invoices**: After successful payment, you can view and download electronic invoices in the WeChat Official Account/Mini Program under "Me - Electronic Invoices."
- **Paper Invoices/Lists**: If a paper version is required, you can print it at the "Self-service Invoice Printing Machine" in the Outpatient Hall using your electronic voucher, or handle it at the manual billing window.

# 5. Emergency and Special Services
*This section addresses urgent situations and the needs of special populations.*

## 5.1 Emergency Guide
- **Emergency Location**: [Hospital North Gate; turn right after entering to see the Emergency Building].
- **Opening Hours**: Open 24 hours a day, 365 days a year.
- **Scope of Emergency**: Only for acute trauma, high fever, acute abdominal pain, cardiovascular/cerebrovascular emergencies, poisoning, coma, and other critical conditions. For chronic diseases, please register for the outpatient clinic.
